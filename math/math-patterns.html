Patterns and sequences

What are patterns and sequences?

Patterns and sequences in math refer to ordered sets of numbers, shapes, or other objects that follow a specific rule or set of rules. Recognizing and understanding patterns and sequences are fundamental skills in mathematics that help develop problem-solving abilities and logical thinking.

Types and rules of patterns and sequences:

  • Arithmetic Sequences: A sequence of numbers where the difference between consecutive terms is constant. an = a1 + (n - 1 ) * d
  • an is the nth term, you want to find
  • a1 is the first term
  • d is the common term

    • Example: Let's say we have the arithmetic sequence: 3, 7, 11, 15, ... The difference between the first two terms is 7 âˆ’ 3 = 4.

    Now, let's find the 5th term (a5):

  • a5 = a1 + (5 - 1) * d
  • Geometric sequences: A sequence of numbers where each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous term by a constant called the common ratio.

    • an = a1 * r(n - 1 )

  • an is the nth term, you want to find
  • a1 is the first term
  • r is the common ratio

    • Example: 3, 6, 12, 24, ... (Common ratio is 2)

  • Fibonacci sequence: A sequence where each term is the sum of the two preceding ones, usually starting with 0 and 1.

    • F n = F n-1 + F n-2

    F n is the nth term

    F n - 1 is the term before F n

    F n - 2 is the term before F n - 1

    Example: 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, ...

    Quizzes:

    1. Sarah saves $10 each week. If she starts with $5, how much will she have saved after 4 weeks?

    2. A bacteria culture doubles every hour. If there is 1 bacterium at the start, how many will there be after 5 hours?

    3. If a pair of rabbits produces another pair every month, and the pair matures in one month, how many pairs of rabbits will there be after 6 months starting with one pair?

    4. A pattern of shapes goes: triangle, square, pentagon, hexagon. What is the next shape in the pattern?

    5. A train schedule shows that trains arrive every 15 minutes starting at 8:00 AM. If the pattern continues, what time will the next train arrive after 9:00 AM?
