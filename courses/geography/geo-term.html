Key Terms in Geography

Key Terms in Geography

  1. Absolute Location - The exact position of a place on the Earth’s surface, expressed in degrees of latitude and longitude.
  2. Relative Location - A place's position in relation to other locations (e.g., "north of the river").
  3. Latitude - Imaginary lines running horizontally around the Earth, used to measure distances north or south of the Equator.
  4. Longitude - Imaginary lines running vertically from pole to pole, used to measure distances east or west of the Prime Meridian.
  5. Equator - An imaginary line that divides the Earth into northern and southern hemispheres, located at 0° latitude.
  6. Prime Meridian - The line of 0° longitude, which passes through Greenwich, England, and divides the Earth into eastern and western hemispheres.
  7. Hemisphere - Half of the Earth, usually referred to as northern, southern, eastern, or western.
  8. Topography - The physical features of a landscape, including elevation, relief, and landforms.
  9. Climate - The long-term average weather conditions in a particular region.
  10. Weather - The short-term atmospheric conditions in a particular place at a specific time.
  11. Ecosystem - A community of living organisms interacting with each other and their physical environment.
  12. Biosphere - The global sum of all ecosystems, encompassing all living organisms and their environments.
  13. Hydrosphere - All the water on Earth, including oceans, rivers, lakes, and groundwater.
  14. Atmosphere - The layer of gases surrounding the Earth, essential for weather and climate.
  15. Litosphere - The outer layer of the Earth, including the crust and upper mantle, where tectonic activity occurs.
  16. Plate Tectonics - The theory that Earth's outer shell is divided into several plates that glide over the mantle, causing earthquakes, volcanic activity, and the creation of mountains.
  17. Continental Drift - The movement of Earth's continents relative to each other over geological time.
  18. Seismic Activity - Earthquakes and related processes that occur due to the movement of tectonic plates.
  19. Tsunami - A large, powerful sea wave caused by an underwater earthquake, volcanic eruption, or landslide.
  20. Volcano - An opening in the Earth’s crust that allows molten rock, ash, and gases to escape from below the surface.
  21. Archipelago - A group of islands clustered together in a large body of water.
  22. Peninsula - A landmass surrounded by water on three sides but connected to a mainland.
  23. Delta - A landform created by the deposition of sediment at the mouth of a river where it enters a body of water, like an ocean or lake.
  24. Estuary - The area where a freshwater river meets the ocean, creating a rich environment for biodiversity.
  25. Desertification - The process by which fertile land becomes desert, often due to drought, deforestation, or inappropriate agriculture.
  26. Deforestation - The clearing or thinning of forests by humans, leading to environmental degradation and loss of biodiversity.
  27. Rain Shadow - A dry area on the leeward side of a mountain range, caused by the blocking of rain-bearing winds by the mountains.
  28. Biodiversity - The variety of life in the world or in a particular habitat or ecosystem.
  29. Sustainable Development - Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
  30. Urbanization - The increase in the proportion of people living in cities compared to rural areas.
  31. Megacity - A city with a population of over 10 million people.
  32. Suburb - A residential area or community located on the outskirts of a city.
  33. Gentrification - The process of renovating and improving a district so that it conforms to middle-class taste, often displacing lower-income residents.
  34. Demographics - The statistical characteristics of populations, such as age, gender, income, and education.
  35. Population Density - The number of people living per unit of area, typically measured in people per square kilometer or mile.
  36. Carrying Capacity - The maximum population size that an environment can sustain indefinitely, given the available resources.
  37. Migration - The movement of people from one place to another, often for economic, social, or political reasons.
  38. Refugee - A person forced to flee their country due to persecution, war, or violence.
  39. Globalization - The increasing interconnectedness and interdependence of the world’s markets and businesses.
  40. Cultural Diffusion - The spread of cultural beliefs and social activities from one group to another.
  41. Agriculture - The science, art, and practice of cultivating plants and livestock.
  42. Irrigation - The artificial application of water to land to assist in the growing of crops.
  43. Green Revolution - A period during the mid-20th century when new agricultural technologies and practices dramatically increased crop yields.
  44. Fertile Crescent - A historically rich agricultural region in the Middle East where agriculture and early human civilizations flourished.
  45. Monsooon - A seasonal wind pattern that causes heavy rains in parts of South Asia and other regions.
  46. Trade Route - A path or waterway used for the exchange of goods and services between distant places.
  47. Renewable Resources - A natural resource that can be replenished naturally with the passage of time, such as solar energy or timber.
  48. Nonrenewable Resources - A natural resource that cannot be readily replaced by natural means, such as fossil fuels like coal and oil.
  49. Geopolitics - The study of the effects of geography on international politics and relations.
  50. Geographic Information System (GIS) - A system for capturing, storing, analyzing, and managing spatial and geographic data.
