Inequalities

What are inequalities?

Inequalities are mathematical expressions that show the relationship between two values when they are not equal. They tell us if one value is less than, greater than, less than or equal to, or greater than or equal to another value.

< means "less than"

> means "greater than"

≤ means "less than or equal to"

≥ means "greater than or equal to"

Inequality properties:

Inequalities have several important properties that are used when solving them. These properties help maintain the inequality's relationship while manipulating the expressions involved.

  1. Addition and subtraction properties of inequalities: If you add or subtract the same number to both sides of an inequality, the inequality remains true:
    • a < b, then a +c < b + c
    • a < b, then a - c < b -c
    • 3 < 5, then 3 + 4 < 5 + 4
  2. Multiplication and division of inequalities: If you multiply and divide both sides of an inequality by a positive number, the inequality remains true. If you multiply both sides by a negative number, the inequality sign reverses:
    • If a < b and c > 0, then ac < bc
    • If a < b and c < 0, then ac > bc
    • 3 < 5, then 3 x 4 < 5 x 4
  3. Transitive Property of Inequalities: If one quantity is less than a second quantity, and the second quantity is less than a third quantity, then the first quantity is less than the third quantity:
    • If a < b and b < c, then a < c
    • 3 < 5 and 5 < 7, then 3 < 7

  1. Step 1: Isolate the Variable on One Side. To isolate the variable x, we need to get rid of the constant term on the side with the variable. Add 5 to both sides
    • 3x - 5 + 5 < 7 + 12
    • 3x < 12
  2. Step 2: Solve for the Variable. Now, we need to solve for x by isolating it completely. Divide both sides by 3
    • 3x ÷ 3 < 12 ÷ 3
    • x < 4
  3. Step 3: The final result. The result is x < 4
