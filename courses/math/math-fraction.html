FRACTION

What are fractions?

Fractions are like pieces of a whole. Imagine you have a pizza or a cake, and you want to share it with friends. A fraction tells you how much of the whole you have. It has two parts:

- The top number is called the numerator and tells you how many pieces you have.

- The bottom number is called the denominator and tells you how many pieces make up the whole.

Why we need fractions?

Fractions help us understand how to divide things equally and measure amounts when cooking or sharing things with others. There are different types of fractions:

- Proper Fractions: When you have less than a whole, like having two out of three slices of pizza.

- Improper Fractions: When you have more than a whole, like having five out of four slices of pizza (imagine an overflowing pizza!).

- Mixed Fractions: When you combine a whole number with a proper fraction, like having one whole pizza plus a little extra.

- Equivalent Fractions: When different fractions represent the same amount, like 1/2 and 2/4.

Rules of using fractions

Rule #1: Common denominator.

Common Denominator: Before adding or subtracting fractions, make sure their bottom numbers (denominators) are the same. To do this, find the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators and rewrite the fractions with that common denominator.To find the least common multiple (LCM) of two numbers, first list the multiples of each number until you find a common multiple. Then, identify the smallest number that appears in both lists, as this is the LCM. For example, to find the LCM of 4 and 6, list the multiples of each number (4, 8, 12, ...) and (6, 12, 18, ...), and identify 12 as the smallest number in both lists, making it the LCM. Once you have the LCM, you can use it to simplify fractions, add or subtract fractions with different denominators, or solve other mathematical problems.

Rule #2: Multiplying Numerators and Denominators.

Multiplying Numerators and Denominators: When multiplying fractions, simply multiply the top numbers (numerators) together and the bottom numbers (denominators) together. When multiplying fractions, such as 2/3 * 4*5, multiply the numerators (2 and 4) together to get 8, and multiply the denominators (3 and 5) together to get 15. So, the result is 8/15.

Rule #3: Division and Multiplication.

Division and Multiplication: To divide one fraction by another, flip the second fraction (find its reciprocal) and multiply it by the first fraction. Quizzes:

1. If you have a pizza divided into 8 slices and you eat 3 slices, what fraction of the pizza have you eaten?

2. A recipe calls for 3/4 cup of flour, but you only have a 1/2 cup measuring cup. How many times do you need to fill the 1/2 cup measuring cup to get the required amount of flour?

3. You have 12 cookies, and you want to share them equally among 4 friends. How many cookies will each friend get?

4. What is the sum of 1/3 and 1/6?

5. If you have 3/4 of a pie, but you want to give away 1/8 of it, how much pie will you have left?
