Proportions

What are proportions?

Proportions are equations that show two ratios are equal. A ratio is a comparison between two quantities, often written as a:b or a/b . When two ratios are equal, they form a proportion. Proportions help us compare quantities and find unknown values based on those comparisons. They are often used in recipes, maps, and real-life situations where scaling is important.

Rules of solving proportion problems:

Identify the Proportion: Recognize when you have a proportion, which is an equation where two ratios are equal:

a b = c d

Cross Multiply: Multiply the numerator of the first fraction by the denominator of the second fraction, and vice versa.

ad=bc

Example #1:

Imagine you are painting a room and need to mix different amounts of paint colors to achieve the desired shade. You have a can of blue paint that can cover 4 square meters of wall space. You want to find out how much paint you'll need to cover a wall area of 12 square meters.

Step 1: Write the proportion:

1 4 = x 12

Step 2: Cross multiply:

1 * 12 = x * 4

12 = 4x

Step 3: Solve for x

12 4 = 4x 4

3 = x

You need 3 cans of blue to cover a wall area of 12 square meters
