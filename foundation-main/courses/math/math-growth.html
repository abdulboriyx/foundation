Linear vs exponential growth

What are linear and exponential growths?

Linear growth occurs when a quantity increases or decreases by the same amount over equal intervals of time. Exponential growth occurs when a quantity increases or decreases by a constant percentage rate over equal intervals of time.

Differences between linear and exponential growth:

Rate of Increase: In linear growth, the quantity changes by a constant amount over time, while in exponential growth, the quantity changes by a constant percentage rate over time.

Shape of Growth Curve: Linear growth results in a straight line when plotted on a graph, while exponential growth results in a curve that becomes increasingly steep over time.

Magnitude of Growth: Exponential growth accelerates rapidly over time, leading to much larger increases compared to linear growth, especially in the long term.

Formula:

Linear growth: P(t) = P0 + rt

P(t) = Quantity at time t

P0 = Initial quantity (t = 0)

r = Rate of change (constant)

t = Time elapsed

Example:

A company has 100 employees and hires 10 new employees every month. How many employees will company have in 6 months? Solution:

Initial State: Start with 100 employees. (P0)

Monthly increase: Add 10 employees each month. (r)

1. If a car depreciates by $500 per year, what will be its value after 3 years if its initial value is $10,000?

2. If a population of bacteria doubles every hour and starts with 10 bacteria, how many bacteria will there be after 4 hours?

3. If an investment grows at a rate of 5% per year and starts with $1,000, how much will it be worth after 10 years?
