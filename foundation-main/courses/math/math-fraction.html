FRACTION

What are fractions?

A fraction represents a part of a whole or, more generally, any number of equal parts. It consists of two numbers: a numerator and a denominator. The numerator is the number of parts being considered, and the denominator is the total number of equal parts in the whole.

The general form of a fraction is:

numeratordenominator

Types of fractions

  1. Proper fractions: A fraction where the numerator is less than the denominator. For example:

    3 4 , 5 8 , 2 6

  2. Improper fractions: A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator. For example:

    4 3 9 7 7 3

  3. Mixed Numbers: A combination of a whole number and a proper fraction. For example:

    2 2 3 5 4 7 10 7 9

  4. Equivalent Fractions: Different fractions that represent the same value. For example:

    2 3 is equal to 4 6 is equal to 8 12

Rules

Addition

Addition of proper fractions

1 3 + 1 4

  1. Step 1: Find the least common multiple (LCM) of 3 and 4.
    • The multiples of 3 are 3, 6, 9, 12, ...
    • The multiples of 4 are 4, 8, 12, 16, ...
    • The least common multiple (LCM) of 3 and 4 is 12.
  2. Step 2: Rewrite each fraction with the common denominator (12).
    • 1 3 x 4 4 = 4 12
    • 1 4 x 3 3 = 3 12
  3. Step 3: Add the numerators:
    • 4 12 + 3 12 = 7 12
  4. Step 4: Result is 7 12

Addition of improper fractions

7 4 + 5 3

  1. Step 1: Find a common denominator for 4 and 3.

    The least common multiple (LCM) of 4 and 3 is 12.

  2. Step 2: Rewrite each fraction with the common denominator (12).
    • 7 4 x 3 3 = 21 12
    • 5 3 x 4 4 = 20 12
  3. Step 3: Add the numerators.
    • 21 12 + 20 12 = 41 12
  4. Step 4: Result is 41 12

Addition of mixed fractions

3 2 5 + 2 3 4

  1. Step 1: Convert to improper fractions
    • 3 2 5 becomes (3 x 5) + 2 5 = 17 5
    • 2 3 4 becomes (2 x 4) + 3 4
  2. Step 2: Find a Common Denominator.

    The least common multiple (LCM) of 5 and 4 is 20.

  3. Step 3: Rewrite Each Fraction.
    • 17 5 x 4 4 = 68 20
    • 11 4 x 5 5 = 55 20
  4. Step 4: Add the Numerators
    • 68 20 + 55 20 = 123 20
  5. Step 5: Result is 123 20

Subtraction

Subtraction of proper fractions

3 4 - 1 4

  1. Step 1: Common Denominator: Since the denominators are the same, no conversion is needed.
  2. Step 2: Subtract the Numerators.
    • 3 4 - 1 4 = 2 4
  3. Step 3: Simplify (if Necessary): In this case, 2 4 can be simplified to 1 2 by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD), which is 2.
  4. Step 4: Result is 1 2

Subtraction of improper fractions

7 4 - 5 3

  1. Step 1: Find a Common Denominator: Determine the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators, which is 12 in this case.
  2. Step 2: Rewrite Fractions with the Common Denominator
    • 7 4 x 3 3 = 21 12
    • 5 3 x 4 4 = 20 12
  3. Step 3: Subtract the numerators
    • 21 12 - 20 12 = 1 12
  4. Step 4: Result is 1 12

Subtraction of mixed fractions

4 1 3 - 2 2 5

  1. Step 1: Convert to Improper Fractions.
    • 4 1 3 becomes 13 3
    • 2 2 5 becomes 12 5
  2. Step 2: Find a Common Denominator: The least common multiple (LCM) of 3 and 5 is 15.
  3. Step 3: Rewrite Fractions with the Common Denominator:
    • 13 3 x 5 5 = 65 5
    • 12 5 x 3 3 = 36 15
  4. Subtract the Numerator
    • 65 5 - 36 15 = 29 15
  5. Step 5: Result is 29 15

Multiplication

Multiplication of fractions, whether proper, improper, or mixed, involves the same basic steps: multiplying the numerators together to get the new numerator, and multiplying the denominators together to get the new denominator. The result is then simplified if necessary. For mixed fractions, an additional step is required: convert the mixed fractions to improper fractions before performing the multiplication.

3 4 x 2 3

  1. Step 1: Multiply the numerator and denominator.
    • 3 x 2 = 6
    • 4 x 3 = 12
  2. Step 2: Result is 3 4 x 2 3 = 6 12
  3. Step 3: Simplify the result
    • Since both the numerator and denominator share a common factor of 6, divide both by 6
    • 6 12 ÷ 6 6 = 1 2
  4. Step 4: Final result is 1 2

Division

The division of fractions, whether proper, improper, or mixed, follows the same basic process: multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor. This means flipping the second fraction (the divisor) and then multiplying. Simplify the result if necessary.

  1. Step 1: Reciprocal of the Divisor.
    • Reciprocal of 2 5 is 5 2
  2. Step 2: Multiply
    • 3 4 x 5 2 = 15 8
  3. Step 3: Simplify if necessary
    • 15 8 is already in its simplest form, but as an improper fraction, it can also be written as 1 7 8
  4. Step 4: Final result is 15 8 or 1 7 8
