What are numbers?

Numbers are mathematical entities used to represent quantities, values, or positions. They form the basis of mathematics and are used in various contexts, from counting objects to measuring quantities and solving equations.

Types of numbers:

  • Natural numbers: Also known as counting numbers, natural numbers are positive integers starting from 1.

    • Example: 7, 10, 2, 200

  • Whole numbers: Whole numbers include all the natural numbers along with zero.

    • Example: 0, 1

  • Integer numbers: Integers are whole numbers, including negative numbers and zero

    • Example: -100, 0, 100

  • Rational numbers: Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers, where the denominator is not zero.

    • Example: 1/4, 5/2, 0.7

  • Irrational Numbers: Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction and have non-repeating, non-terminating decimal representations.

    • Example:√2, √3
